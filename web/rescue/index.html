What happened

Campfire Ranch was the company that operated Lost Trail Lodge and Thelma Hut. On June 18, 2026 it dissolved and notified guests that upcoming reservations were cancelled.

The properties are owned by Stok Investment Group (SIG) — we're the landlord, and now the operator. Rather than let your trip fall through, we're stepping in to run both lodges ourselves and to honor as many existing bookings as we can.

We know a cancellation email is alarming. Our goal is simple: get you reconfirmed, answer your questions honestly, and make sure your trip still happens.

Questions, answered honestly

Is my reservation really still happening?

That's our intent. We're honoring as many existing bookings as we can. Reconfirm with the form above and we'll lock in your dates and follow up personally.

I already paid Campfire Ranch — what about that money?

Many guests paid Campfire in advance. Those funds were held by Campfire Ranch, not by Stok Investment Group, and we're still working through what transferred. We won't leave you stranded — contact us and we'll work with you individually on applying a credit or arranging payment so your trip happens.

Will my dates or price change?

We're working to keep your original dates. We'll confirm your specifics with you directly when you reconfirm; if anything needs to change, you'll hear it from a human first, not a surprise email.

Who is Stok Investment Group?

SIG owns both properties — Lost Trail Lodge in Coldstream Canyon, California, and Thelma Hut on Red Mountain Pass, Colorado. We were the landlord; we're now the operator, running both lodges directly.

What if I need to cancel or change my trip?

Just tell us. Reconfirm or email us and we'll talk it through — no run-around.

How do I reach a real person?

Use the form above or email us directly at our reservations inbox. A person reads every message.
